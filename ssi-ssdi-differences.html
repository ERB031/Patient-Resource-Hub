Differences between SSI/SSDI

The main differences between SSI (Supplemental Security Income) & SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance) are in eligibility and health insurance coverage. If you have low income/resources and a prior work history, you can qualify for both benefits, however, your benefit won't necessarily increase by receiving both.

Eligibility Differences:

  • SSI provides monthly payments to elderly/disabled people whose income/resources fall below specific financial limits, regardless of their work history.
  • SSDI pays benefits to disabled people who worked long enough and recently enough to pay a certain amount of Social Security taxes on their earnings.

Health Insurance Coverage Differences:

  • SSI recipients automatically qualify for Medicaid upon receipt of SSI (in most states)
  • SSDI recipients automatically qualify for Medicare after a 24-month waiting period from time the disability began
