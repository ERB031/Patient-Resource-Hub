A guide to social determinants of health

Why the standard of care leaves people behind

In a perfect world, “standard of care” would mean that everyone gets evidence-based treatment, respectful communication, and the right follow-up. But the true standard shifts depending on income, disability, race, gender identity, immigration status, geography, and whether the system was built with you in mind.

Use this page to see how social determinants of health shape clinical decisions—and to find language you can bring into appointments, appeals, and community organizing.

  • Skim each primer to understand what the care gap looks like and how patients are protecting themselves.
  • Follow the linked resources for deeper dives, community-created guides, and advocacy toolkits.

A note about intersectionality

Each patient's experience is unique and usually shaped by multiple determinants at once. This list is a starting point—not an exhaustive map of every community's reality.

Take action

Use these moves to shift care in your community:

  • Bring specific language from each determinant guide into appointments, appeals, or advocacy letters.
  • Document bias with dates, locations, and provider names; share the notes with patient advocates or legal aid.
  • Share research from the SOC Learning Materials when you ask institutions for safer protocols.

Get help

Lean on the communities and systems working for you:

  • Connect with mutual aid and justice networks highlighted in the Notion workspace.
  • Ask social workers or case managers about culturally responsive ombudsperson offices at your hospitals.
  • Return to this page for updated experiences and reach out via the Patient Resource Hub workspace when you need collaborators.
