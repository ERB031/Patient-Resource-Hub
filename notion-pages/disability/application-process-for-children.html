Application Process for Children

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc
You can apply in any of the following ways:
  • online application
  • in-person (use the office locator to find an office near you, make sure you call and schedule an appointment before going) - you can use form SSA-3820-BK to prepare
  • by phone (1-800-772-1213 or TTY 1-800-325-0778) Monday to Friday from 8am - 7pm Eastern Time
If you are told to mail documents, do not write anything on the original documents. Write your Social Security number on a separate sheet of paper and include it in the mailing envelope along with the documents so that they can be matched with the correct application.
If you are applying through a lawyer or other representative, they can find forms and information here.
You will need the following information to apply:
If you are missing any of these documents, do not delay applying for benefits. SSA may be able to help you get them.
Information About The Child
Social Security card & Social Security Number (SSN)
Proof of U.S. citizenship or lawful alien status 
  • citizenship: birth certificate, passport, or naturalization certificate
  • lawful alien status: I-551 (Permanent Resident Card), I-94 (arrival/departure record), or other documents
Date and place of birth
  • If the child was born in the US: their original birth certificate
  • If the child was born outside the US: other proof of birth before age 5
Medical Information
Medical evidence already in your possession.
  • medical records, doctors' reports, and recent test results (copies okay)
  • medication list including who prescribed each medicine & what they are for
  • patient ID numbers and contact information for your doctors, hospitals, and clinics
School & Social Services Information
(Use the Medical and School Worksheet to help you with this portion)
Names and contact info for any schools the child attended in the past year
  • List of the teachers, psychologists, counselors, speech, and other therapists who have seen/treated the child
  • Individualized Family Service Plan (IFSP) for early intervention services
  • Individualized Education Program (IEP) for special education services
  • Any other relevant school records
Names and contact info for any social service programs the child has worked with
  • Name(s) of caseworkers that have information about the child
  • Names and contact info for another adult who helps care for the child
Financial & Resource Information
proof of current income for the child and family members living in the household (pay stubs, self-employment tax returns, unemployment or other program benefits, child support, etc.)
proof of resources for the child and parents living in the household (bank account statements, life insurance policies, certificates of deposit, stocks or bonds)
names and contact info for any employers the child has had (if any)
