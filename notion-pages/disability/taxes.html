Taxes

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc

About 1/3 of SSDI beneficiaries pay taxes on their benefits. You’ll be affected only if you have substantial income in addition to your Social Security benefits:

  • If you file a federal individual income tax return and your income is more than $25,000
  • If you file a joint return, you may have to pay taxes if you and your spouse have a combined income that is more than $32,000.
  • If you’re married and file a separate return, you’ll probably pay taxes on your benefits.

SSI is never taxable.
