Interpreters

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc
SSA will provide you with an interpreter free of charge if you request language assistance or when it is evident that such assistance is necessary to ensure that you are not disadvantaged.
 
If you prefer to use your own interpreter, SSA will determine whether the interpreter meets their requirements. Minors cannot be used as interpreters in complex or sensitive matters unless it is clear that they also meet the requirements:
  • able to read & speak fluently in English and your language
  • agrees to provide an exact interpretation of questions and responses by both you and the Social Security interviewer
  • does not self–initiate follow–up questions or infer facts, dates, or other information not provided by you or the Social Security interviewer
  • demonstrates familiarity with basic terminology used in our materials and interviews
  • has no personal stake in the outcome of the case (that would create a conflict of interest)
If your interpreter is deemed to be unqualified, SSA will continue the interview - but only with the additional assistance of an interpreter they provide. This interpreter can be over the phone (same-day) or in-office (will need to be rescheduled).
