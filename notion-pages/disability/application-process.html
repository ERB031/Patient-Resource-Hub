Application Process

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc
You can apply in any of the following ways:
  • online application (tips for applying online available here)
  • in-person (use the office locator to find an office near you - make sure you call and schedule an appointment before going)
  • by phone (1-800-772-1213 or TTY 1-800-325-0778) Monday to Friday from 8am - 7pm Eastern Time
If you are told to mail documents, do not write anything on the original documents. Write your Social Security number on a separate sheet of paper and include it in the mailing envelope along with the documents so that they can be matched with the correct application.
If you are applying through a lawyer or other representative, they can find forms and information here.
You will need the following information to apply:
If you are missing any of these documents, do not delay applying for benefits. SSA may be able to help you get them.
Information About You
Social Security card & Social Security Number (SSN)
Proof of U.S. citizenship or lawful alien status 
  • citizenship: birth certificate, passport, or naturalization certificate
  • lawful alien status: I-551 (Permanent Resident Card), I-94 (arrival/departure record), or other documents
Date and place of birth
  • If you were born in the US: your original birth certificate
  • If you were born outside the US: other proof of birth before age 5
Alternate Contact
Information About Your Family
Name and SSN of current and prior spouse(s)
Beginning and Ending dates of marriage(s), place of marriage(s)
Names and Dates of Birth of children who:
  • are under 18 years old & unmarried
  • are age 18-19 & still attend secondary school full time
  • became disabled before age 22
Medical Information
Medical evidence already in your possession.
  • medical records, doctors' reports, and recent test results (copies okay)
  • medication list including who prescribed each medicine & what they are for
  • patient ID numbers and contact information for your doctors, hospitals, and clinics
Financial & Resource Information
your bank's Routing Transit Number and your account number(s)
bank statements for all checking & savings accounts
certificates of deposit, stocks, or bonds
award letters, pay stubs, settlement agreements, proof of any temporary or permanent workers' compensation-type benefits you received, or other documentation of unearned income
proof of living arrangement
  • list of household members
  • information about household costs for rent, mortgage, food, and utilities (include lease or rent receipt, if applicable)
  • deed or property tax bill
life or disability insurance policies
titles & registrations for vehicles
burial contracts, burial plots, etc.
Work History
W-2 forms(s), payroll stubs, and/or self-employment tax returns for last year (copies okay)
list of the jobs that you've had in the 15 years
  • dates you worked at those jobs
  • job duties
  • name & address of employers
any US military service history: duty and branch, service period dates, and discharge paper(s) (DD-214)
information about your workers' compensation, black lung, and/or similar benefits you filed (or intend to file for) - including both temporary and permanent benefits, annuities, and lump sum payments
your education
  • highhest grade in school completed (date)
  • any special education (school name, city and state)
 
 
your alternate contact should be someone who knows about your medical conditions and can help with your application if needed
