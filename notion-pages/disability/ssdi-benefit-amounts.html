SSDI Benefit Amounts

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc

Your SSDI payment is based on your average covered earnings over a period of up to 35 years, known as your average indexed monthly earnings (AIME). SSA uses the national average wage indexing series to index your earnings and ensure that your benefits reflect the general rise in the standard of living that occurred during your years of work. A formula is then applied to your AIME to calculate your primary insurance amount (PIA) - which is basic figure that SSA uses in setting your actual benefit amount.

Benefits paid to disabled workers and their families are redetermined triennially. You can use one of the online benefits calculator to determine your payment - the maximum monthly payment at full retirement age in 2021 is $3,148 but the average person receives closer to $1,200.

If your family member is receiving benefits on your account, their payment will be dependent on a number of factors. The family maximum is 85% of your Average Indexed Monthly Earnings (AIME). However, it cannot be less than the worker's Primary Insurance Amount (PIA) nor more than 150 percent of the PIA. Click here for more info.
