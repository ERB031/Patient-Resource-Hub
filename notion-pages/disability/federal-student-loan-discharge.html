Federal Student Loan Discharge

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc

If you are totally and permanently disabled and received one of the following loans, you may be eligible for a Total and Permanent Discharge (TPD).

  • William D. Ford Federal Direct Loan (Direct Loan)
  • Federal Family Education Loan (FFEL)
  • Federal Perkins Loan (Perkins Loan)
  • TEACH Grant service obligations

Discharge of your federal student loan will not impact your benefits.

If you are receiving SSI or SSDI, SSA will contact the Department of Federal Student Aid to let them know the date of your next scheduled disability review. The department will then contact you and explain how you may apply for a TPD discharge, but you will not need to submit documentation of your SSA disability determination.

If you receive SSDI or SSI benefits and your next scheduled disability review will be within 5 to 7 years from the date of your most recent SSA disability determination but were not contacted about a TPD discharge, you can apply and provide documentation of your SSA notice of award for SSDI, SSI benefits or a Benefits Planning Query (BPQY form 2459).

More information about TPD and other ways to apply are available here.
