Overpayments

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc
If you receive an overpayment, SSA will issue you a notice explaining the overpayment and ask for a full refund within 30 days.
Overpayments can occur if:
  • your income ends up being more than you estimated
  • your living situation changes
  • your marital status changes
  • you have more resources than the allowable limit
  • you are no longer disabled
  • you fail report a change
  • SSA has incorrect or incomplete information
If you do not make a full refund, SSA may withhold the overpayment at the rate of the lesser of 10% or the entire monthly payment. If you ask for an appeal within 10 days of the date you receive the notice, your payments will continue until SSA makes a determination. If you were overpaid but you believe it was not your fault and you cannot afford to pay it back, you can complete form SSA 632 to request a waiver.
