Barriers to Mental Healthcare

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc
  • Mental and behavioral disorders account for nearly 15% of all years of life lost to disability and premature death (The Lancet)
  • In the United States, jails and prisons hold 10x more mentally ill people than state hospitals (BMJ)
  • 1 in 5 adults in did not receive mental health treatment when they needed it due to cost, stigma, and lack of resources (KFF).

    Cost

    • 42% of patients report that their insurance does not adequately cover behavioral health needs (Harvard Business Review).
    • A behavioral health office visit is 3.5x more likely to be out-of-network than a primary care appointment (RTI)

    Stigma

    • At least 31% of Americans have worried about others judging them for seeking out mental health or substance abuse services (CVN)
    • Doctors commonly assume that a mentally ill patient's physical symptoms are a result of their underlying mental health condition without investigation - which can delay essential medical care (Wiley)

    Lack of Resources

    • The US does not have enough inpatient psychiatric beds available to treat patients in need, and communities have no reliable way to access these shortages (APA)
    • IMD Exclusion: Medicaid doesn’t pay for care provided in “institutions for mental disease” - which are psychiatric facilities with more than 16 beds (NAMI)
