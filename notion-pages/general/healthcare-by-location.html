Healthcare by Location

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

View original Notion doc
  • Rural health networks often have fewer specialists and resources than their urban counterparts, which means patients living in remote areas may have to travel great distances to receive care (RHI)
    • Less than 8% of physicians and surgeons choose to practice in rural areas (RHI)
    • 65% of rural areas had a shortage of primary care physicians (STAT)
    • 54% of rural counties do not have a hospital with obstetrics services (Rural Health Research Gateway)
    • 82% of rural Americans live in a county without a detox provider (Rural Health Research Gateway)
  • At least 400 rural hospitals are at risk of closure (Chartis).
    • In states that did not expand Medicaid under the Affordable Care Act, rural hospitals have lower operating margins (KFF)
    • When underfunded rural hospitals close, the death rate in communities rises by approximately 8.7% (NBER)
  • In low-income school districts, students are less likely to have a school nurse, counseling services, and preventative care screenings (MDH)
  • The United States has 6,500 food deserts (places where economics and geography make it harder for people to access healthy, nutritious food) (Bayer)
