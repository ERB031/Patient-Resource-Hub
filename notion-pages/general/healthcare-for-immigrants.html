Social determinants of health

Healthcare for Immigrants

This page mirrors our Notion doc so you can browse without leaving the site.

Back to standard of care View original Notion doc
  • Like prisons and jails, many ICE detention centers outsource medical care to private companies. Detainees in these centers are subject to abuse, including severe medical neglect (Marshall Project) and forced sterilizations (ACLU).
  • Undocumented folks who are not detained may avoid seeking medical care and other social services for fear of deportation or detention (PMC)
  • Immigrants and refugees are less likely to have access to health insurance (KKF)
  • Patients who don't speak English may struggle to navigate the healthcare system and/or face discrimination from their providers.
    • Translators (if available) cannot always ensure a solid understanding of medical explanations/terminology, and often can't help with paperwork, social work, and other essential services (medecision)
