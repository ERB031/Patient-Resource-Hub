Eligibility

Alaska Medicaid is only available for people in the following categories who meet certain income requirements:
  • children
  • pregnant women
  • parents & caregivers
  • elderly/disabled residents
  • low-income 9-64 year olds who are ineligible for other programs

Home & Community Based Waivers

Individuals who are elderly and/or disabled may qualify for home and community based services and/or full Medicaid coverage through one of these waiver programs:
AK Adults with Physical and Developmental Disabilities
Who can Apply: Adult (21+) Alaskan residents with autism or a developmental/intellectual disability that began before age 21
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, the income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must be disabled and need nursing facility-level care.
Services: adult day services, care coordination, day habilitation, employment services, residential habilitation, respite, environmental modifications, intensive active treatment, meals, residential supported living, specialized medical equipment, specialized private duty nursing, and transportation
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AK0262.zip
Contact: Division of Public Assistance (800-478-7778)
 
AK Alaskans Living Independently (ALI)
Who can Apply: Adults age 65+ or ages 21-64 with physical a disability
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, the income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must need nursing facility-level care.
Services: adult day, care coordination, respite, environmental modification, meals, residential supported living services, specialized medical equipment, specialized private duty nursing, transportation
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AK0261.zip
Contact: Division of Public Assistance (800-478-7778)
 
AK Children with Complex Medical Conditions
Who can Apply: medically fragile individuals ages 0-21
Financial Eligibility:
Level of Care: The child must need nursing facility-level care.
Services: care coordination, day habilitation, employment services, residential habilitation, respite, environmental modifications, meals, nursing oversight and care management, specialized medical equipment, and transportation

AK Individualized Supports Waiver

Eligibility

Alaska Medicaid is only available for people in the following categories who meet certain income requirements:
  • children
  • pregnant women
  • parents & caregivers
  • elderly/disabled residents
  • low-income 9-64 year olds who are ineligible for other programs
