Home & Community Based Waivers

Individuals who are elderly and/or disabled may qualify for home and community based services and/or full Medicaid coverage through one of these waiver programs:
Elderly and Disabled (E&D) Waiver Program.
Who can Apply: Individuals who are elderly and/or disabled
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must be disabled and need nursing facility-level care.
Services:
  • case management
  • homemaker services
  • personal care
  • respite care
  • adult day health
  • adult companion services
  • home delivered meals
  • pest control
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AL0391.zip
Contact: Alabama Department of Senior Services (1-800-243-5463)
 
State of Alabama Independent Living Waiver (SAIL) Program
Who can Apply: Adults 18+ with specific medical diagnoses who meet the criteria for Medicaid coverage in a nursing home but prefer to remain at home
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must meet the nursing facility level of care.
Services:
  • case management/transitional services
  • personal care services
  • environmental accessibility adaptations
  • personal emergency response system
  • medical supplies, minor assistive technology
  • unskilled respite care
  • pest control
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AL0241.zip Contact: Department of Rehabilitation Services (1-844-602-724)
Specific medical diagnoses include, but are not limited to: Quadriplegia, Traumatic Brain Injury, Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis, Multiple Sclerosis, Spinal Muscular Atrophy, Muscular Dystrophy, Severe Cerebral Palsy, Stroke and other substantial neurological impairments, severely debilitating diseases or rare genetic diseases.
 
Home and Community-Based Waiver for Persons with Intellectual Disabilities (ID)
Who can Apply: Individuals ages 3+ who are diagnosed as mentally retarded
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must meet the Intermediate Care Facility for Individuals with Intellectual Disability Criteria and have an I.Q. below 70.
Services:
  • Residential habilitation - group home/other
  • day habilitation
  • prevocational services/supported employment
  • occupational therapy
  • speech, hearing, language therapy
  • physical therapy
  • behavior management
  • companion services
  • respite care
  • personal care
  • environmental accessibility adaptations
  • medical supplies
  • specialized medical equipment
  • skilled nursing
  • community specialists
  • crisis intervention
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AL0001.zip
Contact: Department of Mental Health, Division of Intellectual Disabilities (1-800-361-4491)
 
Technology Assisted (TA) Waiver for Adults Program
Who can Apply: Individuals who were receiving private duty nursing services but are no longer eligible through the EPSDT Program upon turning age 21, must have a tracheostomy or be ventilator dependent and require skilled nursing services
Financial Eligibility: Individuals must be receiving SSI or have income below 300% of the SSI Federal Benefit Rate
Level of Care: The individual must be disabled and need nursing facility-level care.
Services:
  • Private duty nursing (up to 12 hr/day)
  • personal care/attendant services
  • medical supplies
  • assistive technology
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AL0407.zip
Contact: Alabama Department of Senior Services (1-877-425-2243)
 
Living at Home (LAH) Waiver for Persons with Intellectual Disabilities
Who can Apply: Individuals ages 3+ who are diagnosed as mentally retarded or with related conditions
Financial Eligibility: must meet the financial requirement and be eligible for one of the following: SSI, SSI-related protected groups deemed to be eligible for SSI/Medicaid, Low Income Families with Children, Federal/State Adoption Subsidy Individuals
Level of Care: The individual must meet the Intermediate Care Facility/Individuals with Intellectual Disabilities (ICF/IID) level of care with a diagnosis of mental retardation
Services:
  • In-home residential habilitation
  • personal care
  • personal care transportation
  • respite care
  • day habilitation
  • prevocational services
  • occupational therapy
  • physical therapy
  • skilled nursing
  • behavior management
  • environmental accessibility adaptation
  • specialized medical equipment
  • medical supplies
  • supported employment
  • speech and language therapy
  • community specialist
  • crisis intervention
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AL0391.zip
Contact: Department of Mental Health, Division of Intellectual Disabilities (1-800-361-4491)
Alabama Community Transition (ACT) Waiver
Who can Apply: Individuals with disabilities or long-term care illnesses who currently reside in an institution and desire to transition to the home or community setting.
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The Individual must meet criteria for nursing facility level of care and have a medical approval
Services:
  • Case management
  • transitional assistance
  • personal care
  • homemaker
  • adult day health
  • home delivered meals
  • skilled/unskilled respite
  • skilled nursing
  • adult companion
  • home modifications
  • assistive technology
  • personal emergency response systems
  • medical equipment, supplies, and appliances
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AL0878.zip
Contact: Alabama Department of Senior Services (1-877-425-2243)

Other Programs

One Door Alabama
Through the “One Door Alabama” Initiative multiple Alabama state and community agencies coordinate to ensure that regardless of which agency people go for help, they can access information and one-on-one counseling about the options available across all the agencies and in their communities, including programs such as:
SenioRx
The SenioRX prescription program helps individuals obtain free or low cost brand name prescriptions from pharmaceutical companies. Persons who qualify for SenioRx receive a 3-month supply of prescriptions from various pharmaceutical companies free or at a low cost and can be renewed as long as the person is eligible.
Who Can Apply: adults age 55+ (or Social Security disabled individuals who are under 55 and in the 24-month Medicare waiting period) who have a chronic medical condition and no prescription drug insurance coverage
 
Alabama Cares
The Alabama Cares Program provides services to assist Caregivers with the duties involved in taking care of their loved ones
Nutrition Support
Congregate & Home Delivered Meals
Each county in Alabama has a minimum of one senior center delivering a healthy hot meal, social activities, and access to information from the community Monday - Friday.
Home-Delivered Meals (either a hot meal delivered daily, or a frozen box of 5 meals delivered weekly) are provided to an eligible client at the client’s place of residence by the contract food vendor.
Senior Farmer’s Market Nutrition Program
The Senior FMNP provides coupons to low-income seniors to purchase eligible foods (fruits, vegetables, honey, and fresh-cut herbs) from farmers at local farmers markets and roadside stands.
Application: https://onedoor.alabama.gov/Home/Network
Contact: 800-243-5463
ageline@adss.alabama.gov
Personal Choices
"Personal Choices" is an option for individuals who are part of a HCBS Waiver Program to be provided with a monthly allowance for services (hiring someone to help with their car, saving money for equipment purchases, etc.)
Financial counselors are available to guide you through the process, including developing a budget to help manage the funds designated for care.
Contact:
E&D, HIV/AIDS, or ACT waiver participants: contact the Agency on Aging (1-877-425-2243)
SAIL waiver participants: contact Alabama Department of Rehabilitative Services (1-844-602-7275)
ID or LAH waiver participants: contact Alabama Department of Mental Health at 1-800-361-4491

Other Programs

One Door Alabama
Through the “One Door Alabama” Initiative multiple Alabama state and community agencies coordinate to ensure that regardless of which agency people go for help, they can access information and one-on-one counseling about the options available across all the agencies and in their communities, including programs such as:
SenioRx
The SenioRX prescription program helps individuals obtain free or low cost brand name prescriptions from pharmaceutical companies. Persons who qualify for SenioRx receive a 3-month supply of prescriptions from various pharmaceutical companies free or at a low cost and can be renewed as long as the person is eligible.
Who Can Apply: adults age 55+ (or Social Security disabled individuals who are under 55 and in the 24-month Medicare waiting period) who have a chronic medical condition and no prescription drug insurance coverage

The Alabama Cares Program provides services to assist Caregivers with the duties involved in taking care of their loved ones

Congregate & Home Delivered Meals

Home-Delivered Meals (either a hot meal delivered daily, or a frozen box of 5 meals delivered weekly) are provided to an eligible client at the client’s place of residence by the contract food vendor.

The Senior FMNP provides coupons to low-income seniors to purchase eligible foods (fruits, vegetables, honey, and fresh-cut herbs) from farmers at local farmers markets and roadside stands.

Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
