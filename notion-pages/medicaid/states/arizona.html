Home & Community Based Waivers

Individuals who are elderly and/or disabled may qualify for home and community based services and/or full Medicaid coverage through one of these waiver programs:
Arizona Long Term Care System (ALTCS)
Who can Apply: Individuals age 65+ or who have a disability
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The Individual must meet criteria for nursing facility level of care as determined by AHCCCS or the level of care provided in an Intermediate Care Facility for individuals with intellectual disabilities (ICF-IID)
Services: attendant care; day treatment and training; employment support services; habilitation; home health aide; home nursing; respiratory therapy; respite; therapies (occupational, physical, speech); and transportation.
Application: https://www.azahcccs.gov/Members/Downloads/Publications/DE101andDE202.pdf
https://www.azahcccs.gov/Members/Downloads/Publications/DE-828_english.pdf
Contact: 888-621-6880
 
 

Other Programs

Children’s Rehabilitative Services (CRS)
Children’s Rehabilitative Services (CRS) is a designation given to certain AHCCCS members who have qualifying health conditions.
Cost: Families whose annual incomes over 150% FPL may be required to pay a monthly premium based on income. Total out-of-pocket cost sharing will not exceed 5% of income.
Eligibility: The child must
  • be under age 21
  • have a qualifying medical condition
Application: https://www.azahcccs.gov/PlansProviders/Downloads/CRSApplicationEnglish.pdf
https://www.azahcccs.gov/PlansProviders/Downloads/CRSApplicationInstructions.pdf
Contact: CRS Unit: 602-417-4545 or 1-855-333-7828
Well Woman HealthCheck Program
This program helps low-income, uninsured, and underinsured women gain access to breast and cervical cancer screening and diagnostic services, including: clinical breast exams, mammograms, pap tests, and pelvic exams. Diagnostic testing is such as ultrasounds, biopsies, other tests are also available, if screening results are abnormal. Referrals are made for treatment as necessary.
Eligibility: uninsured & underinsured Arizona residents AFAB w/ income below 250% FPL
  • breast cancer screenings: ages 40-64
  • cervical cancer screenings: ages 21-64
Contact: 1-888-257-8502
Breast and Cervical Cancer Treatment Program (BCCTP)
Uninsured Arizona women diagnosed with breast or cervical cancer on or after August 2, 2012, may be able to receive comprehensive treatment through the Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System (AHCCCS).
Eligibility: Arizona residents who are:
  • diagnosed with breast cancer, cervical cancer, or pre-cancerous cervical lesions
  • uninsured
  • under age 65
  • U.S. Citizen or a non-citizen qualified to receive Medicaid
  • income under 250% FPL
  • not eligible for AHCCCS (Medicaid) through any other program
Application: https://www.azdhs.gov/documents/prevention/tobacco-chronic-disease/cancer-prevention-control/bcctp/bc-100.pdf
Contact: (602) 542-4611

Individuals who are elderly and/or disabled may qualify for home and community based services and/or full Medicaid coverage through one of these waiver programs:
Arizona Long Term Care System (ALTCS)
Who can Apply: Individuals age 65+ or who have a disability
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The Individual must meet criteria for nursing facility level of care as determined by AHCCCS or the level of care provided in an Intermediate Care Facility for individuals with intellectual disabilities (ICF-IID)
