  • pregnant women
  • parents & caregivers
  • low-income adults

Home & Community Based Waivers

Individuals who are elderly and/or disabled may qualify for home and community based services and/or full Medicaid coverage through one of these waiver programs:
AR Alternatives for Adults w/ Physical Disabilities
Who can Apply: physically disabled individuals ages 21 – 64
Financial Eligibility:
Level of Care:
Services: counseling support management, attendant care, environmental accessibility adaptations/adaptive equipment
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AR0312.zip
Contact: Intake & referral department (501-683-5687)
AR Autism
Who can Apply: children who have been diagnosed with autism ages 1-7
Financial Eligibility:
Level of Care: The individual must meet the Intermediate Care Facility for Individuals with Intellectual Disability Criteria
Services: consultative clinical and therapeutic services, individual assessment/treatment development/monitoring, lead therapy intervention, line therapy intervention, and therapeutic aides and behavioral reinforcers
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AR0936.zip
Contact: 800-342-2923
AR Choices in Homecare
Who can Apply: adults age 65+ or ages 21-64 with a physical disability
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must meet nursing home admission criteria at the intermediate level.
Services:
  • adult day health
  • respite
  • adult day services
  • adult family home
  • attendant care services
  • environmental accessibility adaptations/adaptive equipment
  • home-delivered meals
  • personal emergency response system (PERS)
  • prevocational services
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AR0195.zip
Contact: 888-682-0044
 
AR Community and Employment Support
Who can Apply: individuals of any age who have been diagnosed with autism, intellectual disability, or developmental disability.
Financial Eligibility:
Level of Care: The individual must meet the Intermediate Care Facility for Individuals with Intellectual Disability Criteria.
Services: caregiver respite, supported employment, supportive living, specialized medical supplies, adaptive equipment, community transition services, consultation, crisis intervention, environmental modifications, supplemental support
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AR0188.zip
Contact: Division of Developmental Disabilities Services (501-683-0569)
AR Living Choices Assisted Living Waiver
Who can Apply: Adults age 65+ or ages 21-64 with physical a disability or blindness, must reside in Level II assisted living facilities (ALFs), in apartment-style living units
Financial Eligibility: For individuals, income limit is $2,382 per month and the resource limit is $2,000.
Level of Care: The individual must meet nursing home admission criteria at the intermediate level
Services: extended Medicaid State plan prescription drugs and living choices assisted living service
Application: https://www.medicaid.gov/Medicaid-CHIP-Program-Information/By-Topics/Waivers/Downloads/AR0400.zip

Other Programs

PASSE – Provider-Led Arkansas Shared Savings Entity
PASSE is a program that serves Medicaid clients with complex behavioral health, developmental, or intellectual disabilities. The goal of the PASSE system is to monitor client’s health care needs, keep them healthy, and help them reach goals. DHS, AFMC and each PASSE continues to work together internally to address questions or concerns for providers and clients about the PASSE program as they arise.
A Medicaid client’s services are managed and reimbursed by the PASSEs if the person:
  • is on the Developmental Disabilities (DD) Waiver
  • is on the DD Waiver wait list and gets Medicaid state plan services
  • lives in a private DD Intermediate Care Facility
  • has a Behavioral Health (BH) diagnosis and needs services in addition to counseling and medication management
Enrollment in the PASSE program does not impact a person's eligibility for Medicaid.
 
 
Arkansas Works
Arkansas Works is a Medicaid program that offers eligible Arkansans private health insurance coverage through qualified health plans from Arkansas BlueCross BlueShield, Arkansas Health & Wellness (Ambetter), and Qualchoice:
  • Arkansas BlueCross BlueShield
  • Arkansas Health & Wellness (Ambetter)
  • Qualchoice
Arkansans ages 19-64 who have a household income of up to 138% of Federal Poverty Level (FPL) for their household size may be eligible for Arkansas Works.
Tax Equity and Fiscal Responsibility Act (TEFRA)
The Tax Equity and Fiscal Responsibility Act (TEFRA) is a Medicaid program that can help families with children younger than 19 years old who have a disability receive care in their homes rather than an institution.
Cost: Families whose annual incomes over 150% FPL may be required to pay a monthly premium based on income. Total out-of-pocket cost sharing will not exceed 5% of income.
Eligibility: A child must:
  • meet the Social Security Administration’s (SSA) definition of disabled
  • have a medical condition that would require institutional placement in a hospital, skilled nursing facility, Intermediate Care Facility for Individuals with Intellectual Disabilities (ICF/IID), Alternative Home placement, or be at risk for future institutional placement.
  • the child cannot have personal income that exceeds $2,382 per month or resources worth over $2,000.
Application: https://humanservices.arkansas.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020_TEFRA_ApplicationPacket.pdf
Renewal Application: https://humanservices.arkansas.gov/wp-content/uploads/2019_TEFRA_RenewalPacket.pdf
(You must renew your application every year to remain in the TERFA program.)
Contact: https://humanservices.arkansas.gov/divisions-shared-services/medical-services/healthcare-programs/tefra/tefra-important-phone-numbers/
 
 
First Connections
First Connections offers supportive services to families of children (0 to 3) with developmental delay or disability and links families to resources in the community to help them better advocate for their child at no cost.
Cost: Families whose annual incomes over 150% FPL may be required to pay a monthly premium based on income. Total out-of-pocket cost sharing will not exceed 5% of income.
Eligibility: A child must:
  • have a medically diagnosed physical or mental condition which has a high probability of resulting in developmental delay
  • be experiencing a significant delay in one or more of the following areas of development due to their developmental disability:
    • Physical (gross and fine motor skills)
    • Hearing and Vision
    • Cognitive
    • Communication
    • Social or Emotional
    • Adaptive Skills
Application: https://dhs.arkansas.gov/dds/firstconnectionsweb/#fc-referrals
Contact: 1-800-643-8258 or 1-501-682-8158
 
 
Self Direction/IndependentChoices
Self-direction promotes personal choice and control over the delivery of waiver and state plan services, including who provides the services and how services are provided. There are currently 2 programs offering the self-direction model:
IndpendentChoices (1915i State Plan Services)
ARChoices (1915c HCBS Waiver Services)
They provide assistance with activities of daily living such as bathing, dressing, walking, or toileting and allows you or your representative the opportunity to hire the person who will provide the needed services. Medicaid provides the funding, but you are responsible to ensure the needed services are provided.
Participants using the self-direction model will work with a Financial Management Service (FMS) vendor who has contracted with DHS. The FMS vendor is not a provider and does not have Employer Authority. This FMS vendor assists individuals in exercising their budget authority. DHS is currently contracted with Palco, Inc. as the FMS vendor for the state of Arkansas.

Other Programs

PASSE – Provider-Led Arkansas Shared Savings Entity
PASSE is a program that serves Medicaid clients with complex behavioral health, developmental, or intellectual disabilities. The goal of the PASSE system is to monitor client’s health care needs, keep them healthy, and help them reach goals. DHS, AFMC and each PASSE continues to work together internally to address questions or concerns for providers and clients about the PASSE program as they arise.
A Medicaid client’s services are managed and reimbursed by the PASSEs if the person:
  • is on the Developmental Disabilities (DD) Waiver
  • is on the DD Waiver wait list and gets Medicaid state plan services
  • lives in a private DD Intermediate Care Facility
  • has a Behavioral Health (BH) diagnosis and needs services in addition to counseling and medication management
Enrollment in the PASSE program does not impact a person's eligibility for Medicaid.
 
 
Arkansas Works
Arkansas Works is a Medicaid program that offers eligible Arkansans private health insurance coverage through qualified health plans from Arkansas BlueCross BlueShield, Arkansas Health & Wellness (Ambetter), and Qualchoice:
  • Arkansas BlueCross BlueShield
  • Arkansas Health & Wellness (Ambetter)
  • Qualchoice
Arkansans ages 19-64 who have a household income of up to 138% of Federal Poverty Level (FPL) for their household size may be eligible for Arkansas Works.
Tax Equity and Fiscal Responsibility Act (TEFRA)
The Tax Equity and Fiscal Responsibility Act (TEFRA) is a Medicaid program that can help families with children younger than 19 years old who have a disability receive care in their homes rather than an institution.
Cost: Families whose annual incomes over 150% FPL may be required to pay a monthly premium based on income. Total out-of-pocket cost sharing will not exceed 5% of income.
Eligibility: A child must:
  • meet the Social Security Administration’s (SSA) definition of disabled
  • have a medical condition that would require institutional placement in a hospital, skilled nursing facility, Intermediate Care Facility for Individuals with Intellectual Disabilities (ICF/IID), Alternative Home placement, or be at risk for future institutional placement.
  • the child cannot have personal income that exceeds $2,382 per month or resources worth over $2,000.

Self Direction/IndependentChoices
Self-direction promotes personal choice and control over the delivery of waiver and state plan services, including who provides the services and how services are provided. There are currently 2 programs offering the self-direction model:
IndpendentChoices (1915i State Plan Services)
ARChoices (1915c HCBS Waiver Services)
They provide assistance with activities of daily living such as bathing, dressing, walking, or toileting and allows you or your representative the opportunity to hire the person who will provide the needed services. Medicaid provides the funding, but you are responsible to ensure the needed services are provided.
Participants using the self-direction model will work with a Financial Management Service (FMS) vendor who has contracted with DHS. The FMS vendor is not a provider and does not have Employer Authority. This FMS vendor assists individuals in exercising their budget authority. DHS is currently contracted with Palco, Inc. as the FMS vendor for the state of Arkansas.
